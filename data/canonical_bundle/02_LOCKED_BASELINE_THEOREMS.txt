==SECTION:: 02.locked.compact_factorization_shape ==

The compact characteristic-polynomial closures at r=2, s = 0.55 and
s = 0.60 exhibit the same shape across both unit and full models:

  unit r=2 :  p_S(y) = (y - L0) * (y - L1) * Q(y)^2
  full r=2 :  p_S(y) = (y - L0) * (y - L1)^2 * C(y)^2

This uniformity is structurally explained at r=2 by two distinct
Verified Fact theorems:

  unit r=2 explained by reversal-involution decomposition
              [ref: 04.structural.unit.reversal]
  full r=2 explained by equitable-partition / quotient-residual
              decomposition  [ref: 04.structural.full.equitable]

The two mechanisms are distinct as currently witnessed. A unified
mechanism in the narrow sense is closed [ref:
04.structural.unified.narrow], but generic structural distinctness is
also closed [ref: 04.structural.distinctness.generic].

The statement "this shape holds at any r=2 s-value not yet tracked"
follows from the abstract-matrix-level structural theorems plus the
slice-independence of the abstract matrix structure recorded in
[ref: 03.abstract.r2.unit_S] and [ref: 03.abstract.r2.full_T],
PROVIDED the same canonical substitution P_i(t) and A(t) through E(t)
applies to the new s-value.

The statement "this shape holds at r=3 or at cross-level" is NOT
established and is NOT a locked baseline item. Both r=2 structural
theorems are explicitly r=2 only.
