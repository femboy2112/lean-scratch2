==SECTION:: 07.interpretation.significance ==

Mathematical significance of the closed material. Each entry is a
statement of what the closure means and what it does NOT mean.

--SUBSECTION:: 07.interpretation.r2_descent_failure --

The descent-to-Q negative closures at the four r=2 non-rational slices
establish that the B-level characteristic polynomial does not lie in
Q[x]. The proof at each slice isolates one specific coefficient, the
x^5 coefficient at unit models or the x^8 coefficient at full models,
and exhibits it as an explicit expression containing the irrational
factor h.

  does establish :
    - the polynomial lives genuinely over the algebraic extension,
      not over Q itself
    - rational-coefficient phenomenon at r=2 is isolated to s=0.50

  does NOT establish :
    - any compact factorization at the failing slice (compact closure
      is independent and remains Verified Fact at S-level)
    - any singularity, rank drop, or zero mode (those closures are
      independent and remain Verified Fact)
    - any Collatz-level conclusion

--SUBSECTION:: 07.interpretation.r2_structural_theorems --

The two r=2 structural theorems explain the compact factorization
shapes at the abstract-matrix level by distinct Verified Fact
mechanisms.

  unit explanation : reversal involution, eigenspace decomposition,
                     factor multiplicity from two independent 3D blocks

  full explanation : equitable partition, cell-constant quotient
                     plus zero-sum residual, factor multiplicity from
                     residual-block structure

  unified status   : narrow shared architecture closed
                     [ref: 04.structural.unified.narrow]
                     generic distinctness closed
                     [ref: 04.structural.distinctness.generic]

  does establish :
    - exact symbolic explanations for the compact factorization shapes
      across all four non-rational r=2 slices
    - block-multiplicity origin of the squared compact factors

  does NOT establish :
    - that either mechanism transfers to r=3
    - that compact-factor multiplicity equals numerical eigenvalue
      multiplicity (separable-factors argument required)
    - any Collatz-level conclusion

--SUBSECTION:: 07.interpretation.r3_generic_rank --

The r=3 generic rank closure (rank 18 unit, rank 27 full) is a strong
structural result and a weaker numerical one.

  does establish :
    - determinant polynomials det(S_{unit,r3}(t)),
      det(S_{full,r3}(t)) are not identically zero in Z[t]
    - generic full rank over Q(t)

  does NOT establish :
    - det(S(t)) != 0 for every real s > 0 (a polynomial in t can be
      generically nonzero and still vanish at finitely many specific
      values t = 2^{-s})
    - any characteristic polynomial; characteristic polynomial requires
      its own witness
    - any subdominant eigenvalue data; Perron closure identifies only
      the dominant eigenvalue

--SUBSECTION:: 07.interpretation.r3_unit_s050_charpoly --

The unit r=3, s=0.50 exact characteristic polynomial closure is the
first slice-specific exact characteristic polynomial closure at r=3.

  does establish :
    - degree-18 exact charpoly with rational coefficients for this
      specific specialization t = sqrt(2)/2
    - rank, kernel, zero-mode, row-sum factor, and hash-verifiable
      reproducibility at this slice

  does NOT establish :
    - descent-to-Q at any other r=3 slice (the r=2 history shows
      s=0.55 and s=0.60 failed descent there)
    - compact factorization at r=3
    - anything about the full r=3 s=0.50 charpoly (full is a separate
      27-dim object) [v2.1 boundary; closed at v2.2 via independent
      witness; see r3_full_s050_charpoly subsection below]
    - cross-level invariance even if this charpoly shares factors with
      the unit r=2 s=0.50 charpoly; any such sharing would be
      Computational Observation only

--SUBSECTION:: 07.interpretation.r3_full_s050_charpoly --

The full r=3, s=0.50 exact characteristic polynomial closure (v2.2,
[ref: 05.charpoly.full.s050]) is the largest exact spectral closure
produced in this canonical to date.

  does establish :
    - exact integer factorization of P_A at the integer-cleared
      matrix A: P_A = (x-R) L^2 C^2 D^2 over Z[x] with explicit L
      monic linear (root 7720456389019941), C monic irreducible
      cubic, D monic irreducible degree 9
    - minimal polynomial m_A = (x-R) L C D over Z[x] of degree 14,
      from real-symmetry diagonalizability
    - certified spectral gap gap_A > 5259845921943359
    - certified positivity of every eigenvalue (from exact rational
      Sturm root isolation, not from "positive entries imply positive
      spectrum" which would be wrong)
    - matrix provenance: A = 2^{54} S_full,r3(2^{-1/2}) = R B_full,r3(0.50)
      with R = 3 (2^{27} - 1)^2 = 54043194723139587
    - corresponding scaling identities for S = A/q and B = A/R

  does NOT establish :
    - exact radical forms for the roots of C or D
    - rationality of the roots of C or D
    - descent-to-Q in the r=2 sense at any other r=3 slice; the
      rationality of P_A coefficients at s=0.50 mirrors the r=2
      pattern at s=0.50 specifically
    - compact factorization in the canonical r=2 c-scaled sense;
      the (x-R) L^2 C^2 D^2 shape is over Z[x], not the c-rescaled
      compact form
    - any cross-level invariance with the unit r=2 s=0.50 closure
      even if specific factor-shape coincidences are observed
    - any Collatz-level conclusion

  significance for next targets :
    The integer-cleared full r=3 closure now anchors slice s=0.50.
    Comparison with the unit r=3 s=0.50 closure
    [ref: 05.charpoly.unit.s050] is now Computational Observation
    available; whether the unit factorization divides the full
    factorization (as it does at r=2 s=0.50) is an open question
    that the next round may scope.

--SUBSECTION:: 07.interpretation.r2_compact_factorization_shape --

Uniformity of compact factorization shape across r=2 non-rational
slices is structurally explained at r=2 by the two structural theorems.

  does establish :
    - structural origin of the shape at r=2
    - extension to any r=2 s-value not yet tracked PROVIDED the same
      canonical substitution applies

  does NOT establish :
    - the shape persists at r=3
    - the shape implies cross-level invariance

==SECTION:: 07.interpretation.boundaries ==

Hard boundaries against false inference. Every audit and every
stakeholder summary must respect these boundaries.

  boundary.no_collatz_proof :
    No closure in this canonical proves or disproves the Collatz
    conjecture. The closures concern exact characteristic-polynomial
    structure of Gram matrices arising from the canonical
    construction. They are not by themselves a Collatz argument.
    Statements suggesting otherwise are Advisory Only at best.

  boundary.no_cross_level :
    r=2 closures do not transfer to r=3. r=3 closures do not transfer
    to other levels. Cross-level invariance requires independent
    witnesses at each level being compared.

  boundary.no_uniformity_to_unknown_slices :
    Closures at the tracked s-values do not establish behavior at
    any other s-value. Each slice requires its own witness.

  boundary.no_uniformity_to_unknown_models :
    Closures for the smaller direct package do not transfer to the
    shifted/doubled package. The shifted/doubled package is
    noncanonical unless explicitly opened as a separate branch.

  boundary.no_eigenvalue_from_factor_multiplicity :
    Compact-factor multiplicity (Q(y)^2, C(y)^2, (y - L1)^2 in r=2
    closures) is not numerical eigenvalue multiplicity. Conversion
    requires separable-factors witness.

  boundary.no_exact_from_numerical :
    Numerical solver output is Computational Observation. It is not a
    substitute for symbolic witness and does not upgrade any Not
    Established item.

  boundary.no_canonical_from_advisory :
    Meta and advisory analysis is Advisory Only. It does not become
    canonical and does not alter conventions or closure status.
